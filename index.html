ABOUT

Everything In All is the recording project of songwriter and multi-instrumentalist Steven Horkey, a talented and compelling musician who seamlessly blends indie, folk, rock, electronic, and classical elements into a lush original style. Based in Tucson, Arizona, Steven writes, performs, and sings all of his songs while occasionally featuring other musicians. His upcoming album Territory and single “Forward Lines” both display his unique skills and creative vision and are a good point of entry to his musical world. Previously, he released two EPs: Catalina and Maps. Everything In All’s songs convey concepts and struggles within the fields of psychology, nature, spirituality, and philosophy, giving them depth and substance rendered in a myriad of powerful vocal harmonies, guitar arrangements, and textures of interwoven melody.

Horkey is an only child who began playing guitar at age 12 and worked his way through different genres and instruments, finding value in them all. Everything In All is his way of integrating all of the music, emotions, fields of study, and self-reflection that have made him the artist he is today. It’s the most meaningful and authentic work of Horkey’s career and he knows it. “I love it because it just feels so totally me,” he said, continuing, “It simply feels right, like I'm aligned with myself, like it's what I am here to do in some serious way.”

Listeners of Everything In All pick up on subtleties and tones that often remind them of acts like Bon Iver and John Frusciante, but the music is far more nuanced than such comparisons can convey. “I'm interested in life’s big questions, and how we make the most of our time here as we evolve both individually and collectively,” Horkey says. When not working on music, he spends his time focused on personal development, meditation, yoga, reading, hiking, and academic research in the fields of psychology and neuroscience. Horkey admits, “My other interests contribute to what I think are the important questions amongst the human experience. As such, I don’t think any part of who I am and what I do doesn’t show up in my music. I would love for my work to support people's lives in all the ways other artists do for myself.”

Above all else, Everything In All’s music is done for personal expression and to sincerely reflect the abounding mystery of life. Anyone who respects that kind of artistic mentality and approach should tune in and follow along for the ride.
